A slower way to arrive.

A gentle checklist for check-in day: timing, light, and the first 10 minutes that set the mood.

Another House March 2026

Photography by Another House

There is a particular quality to arrivals that sets the tone for everything that follows. At Another House, we have learned that the first ten minutes matter more than the first hour. Here is what we have found works.

The art of unhurried arrival

When you step through our doors, we ask that you do not rush. Leave your schedule at the threshold. The rooms are designed for a slower kind of living — not to be consumed in a single evening, but to be savoured, piece by piece, like a long letter from someone who took their time writing it.

"The room should feel like a gentle exhale — the moment you stop performing and start being."

Start with the light. Notice how it moves through the space at different hours. In the morning, it arrives soft and lateral. By afternoon, it pools in unexpected corners. In the evening, it retreats slowly, leaving traces of amber on the walls before the lamps take over.

A practical checklist

Before you do anything else:

  • Set down your bags and leave them by the door — they can wait.
  • Open the windows. Let the outside in for ten minutes.
  • Choose your first surface: a chair, the bed, the floor. Sit with the room before you fill it with activity.
  • Notice three things: a texture, a sound, a scent.
  • Make one small decision: where will you have your first cup of something?

The first ritual

We leave a small selection of teas and a kettle in every room. This is intentional. The ritual of boiling water, of waiting, of holding something warm — it is a punctuation mark between the world you left and the world you are entering.

Take your time with it. The room is not going anywhere. Neither, for now, are you.
